Methodology

Data. Daily queue history from validatorqueue.com (open dataset by etheralpha, 1,100+ days), cross-checked against a public Ethereum beacon node: entry queue vs the sum of pending_deposits, exit queue vs the effective balances of active_exiting validators. When the sources disagree materially (>10% and >50K ETH), the disagreement is flagged here and nothing new is published until they reconcile.

Surge detection. Queue levels are sticky, so we detect on daily inflows: a surge fires when a day's queue growth is ≥3 standard deviations above the trailing 30-day inflow baseline and clears an absolute floor (75K ETH for exits, 150K for entries) so flat regimes can't alarm on noise. Consecutive trigger days merge into one episode. Backtested against the known events of Sept 2025 and Feb–Jun 2026: all caught, fewer than five unmatched episodes per year.

Attribution. Exiting validators are joined to entities via their on-chain withdrawal credentials, using the community-curated entity labels in Dune's public spellbook (hildobby's staking datasets). A label-based match is always published as probable, never confirmed; events with no dominant mapping stay unattributed — the number is published without the name, never the reverse.

Consolidation caveat. Since Pectra (EIP-7251), a batch of exits can be validators consolidating into larger compounding validators — stake reorganizing, not leaving. Attributed exit surges may include consolidation churn; briefs call this out where the evidence allows.
